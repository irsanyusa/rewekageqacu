From Die Hard to Farewell: Paying Tribute to Bruce Willis Unforgettable Contributions

Publish date: 2024-08-25

Bruce Willis doesn’t need any introduction. Most of us grew up with him playing on our TVs, in some action movie that we just couldn’t stop watching. Recently, Bruce’s close family publicly announced that he is retiring, due to a condition called aphasia — and we wanted to delve a bit deeper into the actors’ impressive career and unforgettable contributions.

From his adrenaline-pumping portrayal of John McClane in “Die Hard” to his compelling performances spanning various genres, Willis has left an enduring mark on the world of cinema. Let’s find out some cool facts about the man behind the name — Bruce Willis.

Bruce Willis — from TV shows to blockbusters

Fun fact: Bruce Willis — 67 years old — American actor, singer, and film producer — an iconic figure in pop culture, was born in West Germany, in 1955.

His acting career began off-Broadway in the ’70s and Bruce first rose to fame thanks to the TV show Moonlighting, in 1985. More than 30 years later, he managed to be a part of around 100 films and has multiple awards under his belt.

During the actor’s Moonlighting chapter, the Die Hard franchise began in 1988 and Mr. Willis starred in all 5 parts, with the last one coming out in 2013. Bruce Willis will be forever known as John McClane, the quick-witted detective that saves the day — but we won’t spoil it for you — this is the perfect time to rewatch the good old classics.

Interesting facts we didn’t know about Bruce Willis

CAP/RFS/Capital Pictures/East News

In his first 20 years of life, Bruce struggled with stuttering, he said: “I could hardly talk. It took me 3 minutes to complete a sentence. It was crushing for anyone who wanted to express themselves, who wanted to be heard and couldn’t. It was frightening. Yet, when I became another character, in a play, I lost the stutter. It was phenomenal.”

Bruce Willis is also a singer, his debut album, The Return of Bruno, was released in 1987, and 2 others followed in 1989 and 2001 — the lead single — Respect Yourself, was a hit.

Courtesy Everett Collection / East News

Willis forever changed salaries in Hollywood. In 1987, when actors like Tom Cruise and Michael J. Fox were getting around $3 million for a movie, 20th Century Fox agreed to pay Willis $5 million for his first role in Die Hard. Other stars took notice and “the day after I signed the deal, every actor in Hollywood’s salary went up to $5 million,” stated Willis.

Private life

From 1987 until 2000, Bruce Willis was married to actress Demi Moore. In 2009, the actor married model Emma Heming. Today, he’s father to 5 daughters, 3 from his previous marriage with Demi — Rumer, Scout, and Tallulah as well as 2 daughters with Emma — Mabel and Evelyn.

Bruce with Demi Moore and their 3 daughters

Bruce Willis has a caring, loving family around him.

Bruce with Emma Heming and their 2 daughters

Recently, Bruce’s ex-wife Demi Moore, his current wife, Emma, and their daughters posted a joint statement on social media, and it has been making waves:

“To Bruce’s amazing supporters, as a family, we wanted to share that our beloved Bruce has been experiencing some health issues and has recently been diagnosed with aphasia, which is impacting his cognitive abilities. As a result of this and with much consideration, Bruce is stepping away from the career that has meant so much to him.”

Aphasia — what is it?

AUG/face to face/FaceToFace/REPORTER/East News, AUG/face to face/FaceToFace/REPORTER/East News

Aphasia, most common among older adults, is a language disorder that can make it difficult for individuals to communicate through words. People who suffer from this condition make mistakes, whether they choose the wrong words, put them together incorrectly, or can’t comprehend them.

84.5% of people have never heard of aphasia, and studies show that more than 75% of people affected by this diagnosis do not return to employment.

Bruce Willis’ final years of work

Bruce Willis has been actively working over the past few years, he appeared in 8 movies just last year. While 2 movies have already been released this year, there are still 8 movies in post-production, and we can’t wait to see them. To Bruce, we wish you a happy retirement and lots of happiness in old age.

We hope his journey toward healing will be strong and without barriers, and we forever thank him for the legacy he’s left behind.
